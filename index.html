About

Story of Wei

Wei's Chinese Medicine & Acupuncture Practice (Intangible Cultural Heritage) Inheritor

Born in a family that has been practicing TCM for seven generations, I am very proud to be taught by my father in TCM theory and Chinese medicine since childhood. I started reading Chinese traditional medical books such as 'Huang Di Nei Jing' (Inner Canon of the Yellow Emperor) and 'Qi Jing Ba Mai', which introduce the philosophical foundations of TCM and the Jing Luo theory since that time.

The Wei's Chinese medicine and acupuncture practice has been passed on in my family for generations and was honored as Intangible Cultural Heritage by the government in China since 2012, due to my family's outstanding contribution to the medical and health service of the city.

As an acupuncturist, I am not satisfied to only follow what I learned from my family, and want to go beyond it: I am dedicated to providing personalized treatment plans that place an emphasis on accurate health evaluations and special treatment plans based on patients' individual needs, and committed to meeting those needs.

We're Here for You

Most people are surprised to find that acupuncture is not painful in the way they expect. The needles are extremely thin (much thinner than injection needles or sewing needles). You may feel a brief, mild sensation — often described as a tiny pinch, a dull ache, or a slight tingling — when the needle is inserted. Once the needle is in place, most patients feel relaxed, and many even fall asleep during treatment. In traditional Chinese medicine, these mild sensations (called De Qi) are actually a good sign — they show the energy is responding as intended.

Acupuncture is believed to have been practiced for more than 2,500 years and has been studied and recommended by many medical researchers. Most of our patients saw an improvement after the first treatment.

Side effects of acupuncture are minimal but may include fatigue, slight bruising, tenderness at needle site, light-headedness and temporary worsening of symptoms.

It differs from case to case, but in general, your first visit will take about 45 minutes and return visits will take about 30 minutes.

Drink some water and eat a healthy and light snack. Choose loose-fitting clothing to wear if you don't want to get changed during the session.

I will warmly welcome each and every patient personally. Then we will talk with you to evaluate if you are suitable for the treatment that day and complete a Patient History form and discuss the treatment plan with you. I regard my patients as my friends and treat them with care and respect.

The Acupuncturist will then guide you to your treatment table for a quick relaxation, and then start the treatment. Try to keep calm and relax as much as possible during the treatment but feel free to let the Acupuncturist know your feelings.

The Acupuncturist will make you a nice tea and have a talk about your personalized health plan, and all your questions will be answered by the Acupuncturist.
